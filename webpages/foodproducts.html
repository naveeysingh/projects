Daal Bhat
Dal Bhat stands as the quintessential and cherished staple of Nepali cuisine, embodying a cultural legacy that extends beyond the realm of a mere meal. Comprising lentil soup (dal) and rice (bhat), this culinary cornerstone is a daily ritual in Nepali households, uniting people across diverse regions and backgrounds. The lentil soup, a concoction of yellow or red lentils infused with aromatic spices and temperings, delivers a rich and flavorful essence. Accompanied by boiled rice—often white or brown—the combination provides a harmonious balance. The meal's completeness extends beyond the dal and bhat to an array of side dishes, including seasonal vegetables, pickles, and occasional servings of meat or fish. Dal Bhat is not just sustenance; it is a symbol of hospitality, shared generously with guests to express warmth and connection. Beyond its daily presence, Dal Bhat takes center stage in festive occasions, becoming a celebration of culture and community. Each region puts its unique spin on this dish, incorporating local ingredients and preferences.

Gundruk
Gundruk, a cherished element of Nepali cuisine, is a traditional fermented leafy green that undergoes a meticulous preparation process, primarily using mustard greens. The leaves are finely shredded, sun-dried, and fermented, resulting in a distinctive tangy and slightly sour flavor. Stored for extended periods, gundruk becomes a valuable staple during seasons when fresh greens are scarce. Its versatility shines in various culinary applications, from contributing a unique sourness to soups to being stir-fried with spices as a side dish. Beyond its culinary significance, gundruk holds cultural importance as a communal activity, with the knowledge of its preparation passed down through generations. Not only a flavorful addition to Nepali meals, gundruk also embodies resourcefulness, preserving tradition and nutritional richness through its fermentation process, making it a symbol of culinary heritage and community bonding.

Price: Rs.250 for a plate

Sel Roti
Sel Roti, a traditional and beloved delicacy in Nepali cuisine, is a deep-fried rice-based doughnut that holds a special place in the hearts and taste buds of the Nepali people. Prepared using a simple yet meticulous process, the main ingredients include rice flour, water, sugar, and sometimes milk. The batter is allowed to ferment overnight, imparting a subtle tanginess to the final product. The circular-shaped dough is then deep-fried until golden brown, resulting in a crispy exterior that encases a soft and slightly chewy inside. Sel Roti is often enjoyed during festive occasions, celebrations, and as a part of morning or afternoon snacks. Its distinctive ring shape and the play of textures make it a delightful treat, particularly when paired with yogurt, achar (pickle), or tea. This iconic snack not only satisfies the taste buds but also carries cultural significance, symbolizing togetherness and joy during communal gatherings and festivities in Nepal.

Price: Rs.120 for a plate

Thukpa
Thukpa, a flavorful and heartwarming noodle soup, hails from the mountainous terrains of Tibet but has found a beloved place in Nepali cuisine. This soul-soothing dish is particularly popular in colder regions and offers a comforting blend of rich broth, noodles, and an assortment of vegetables and proteins. The broth, often prepared with a base of meat (such as chicken or mutton) or vegetables, is infused with aromatic spices like garlic, ginger, and sometimes red chili flakes, creating a robust and savory flavor profile. The addition of hand-pulled noodles provides a hearty and satisfying element to the soup. Thukpa is typically garnished with cilantro and green onions and is customizable based on personal preferences, with condiments like soy sauce or hot sauce available for added flavor. This noodle soup not only serves as a delicious and nourishing meal but also reflects the cultural diversity and culinary richness of Nepal, offering a comforting bowl that warms both body and spirit, especially in the crisp mountain air.

Price: Rs.150 for a plate

Yak Cheese
Yak cheese, a unique and prized component of Himalayan cuisine, is crafted from the milk of yaks, the resilient and hardy mountain-dwelling animals. Sourced primarily in the high-altitude regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet, yak cheese boasts a distinctive flavor, often described as rich, nutty, and slightly tangy. Its dense texture and robust taste make it a versatile ingredient, suitable for grating over dishes or enjoyed on its own. Beyond its culinary allure, yak cheese holds cultural significance, representing the resourcefulness of communities in harsh terrains. With its roots deeply embedded in the Himalayan heritage, yak cheese stands as a testament to the adaptability and culinary ingenuity of those living amidst the breathtaking landscapes of the world's highest peaks.

Price: Rs.200 for a plate

Newari Cusine
Newari cuisine, originating from the indigenous Newar community in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal, is a gastronomic delight that reflects centuries-old traditions and a rich cultural heritage. Known for its diverse and intricate flavors, Newari cuisine offers a wide array of dishes that are a feast for both the eyes and the palate. Yomari, a steamed dumpling filled with jaggery and sesame seeds, is a sweet delicacy enjoyed during festivals. Samay Baji, a platter of various foods including beaten rice, fried fish, soybeans, and more, is a staple during celebrations and cultural events. Bara, a popular street food, consists of fried lentil patties often served with yogurt and a variety of pickles. The use of unique spices and ingredients, such as jimbu (a type of dried herb), gives Newari dishes their distinctive taste. The cuisine also features mouthwatering meat-based dishes like Kwati (a soup of various sprouted beans) and various forms of sukuti (dried and spiced meat). Newari cuisine not only tantalizes the taste buds but also plays a vital role in preserving cultural identity, making it an integral part of the diverse culinary landscape of Nepal..

Price: Rs.150 for a plate
